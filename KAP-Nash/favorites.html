Favorite TV Shows

The Jeffersons
The Jeffersons, created by legendary TV writer and producer Norman Lear, aired for eleven seasons from January 1975 to July 1985. The show centered around George and Louise Jefferson, an affluent black couple in New York and owners of a successful dry-cleaning franchise. The sitcom tackled complicated issues, such as illiteracy, racism, and alcoholism while maintaining a balance of comedy and satire. One of my favorite parts of the show was the theme song.
The Twilight Zone
The original Twilight Zone, created by Rod Serling, aired from 1959 from 1964, airing for five seasons. The series covered an eclectic mix of genres, including science fiction, westerns, horror, psychological thriller, fantasy, and dark comedy. Most of the episodes had a surprise outcome, and the ending monologue usually summed up the moral of the story. In 2013, TV Guide ranked the series as #5 in the list of the 60 all-time greatest TV shows. The Twilight Zone is filmed entirely in black and white, and the show's theme music is instantly recognizable.
Big Sky
Big Sky, which is currently appearing weekly on network television, began airing in November 2020. The show is a criminal suspense drama created by David E. Kelley, creator of Big Little Lies and The Practice. Two investigators are searching for three teenage girls who have been kidnapped, and find that one of the kidnappers works in law enforcement. The cast members for this show are very convincing, and each weekly episode ends with a cliffhanger.
60 Minutes
60 Minutes is a weekly news program airing on CBS. The show aired for the first time in 1968, and currently holds more Emmy Awards that any other primetime television program in the United States, with a record of 138 Emmy Awards (as of 2017). 60 Minutes has aired for 53 seasons. The reporting of controversial and provocative news stories, along with inspirational interviews, has informed and influenced generations of viewers. The iconic news show was ranked #6 on TV Guide's list of 50 greatest TV shows of all time.
Super Soul Sunday
Super Soul Sunday is a talk show hosted by Oprah Winfrey on her television channel, the Oprah Winfrey Network. Ms. Winfrey interviews CEOs, entertainers, thought leaders, sports icons, political figures, authors, spiritual mystics, and other influential people. The show debuted in October of 2011, and has continued through 2020. The general theme of Super Soul Sunday is spiritual awareness and enlightenment. The show has an accompanying podcast (Oprah's SuperSoul Conversations) that premiered in 2017, and continues into 2021.
